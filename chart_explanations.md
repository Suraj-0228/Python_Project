# Social Media Analysis - Chart Explanations

This document provides a detailed breakdown of the visualizations generated by the `socialmedia_analysis.py` script.

---

## 1. Average Daily Phone Hours by Occupation (Pie Chart)
- **File Name**: `viz1_occupation_usage.png`
- **What it shows**: This chart represents the distribution of screen time across different professional categories (Student, Professional, Business Owner, Freelancer).
- **Key Insight**: It helps identify which occupation group is most "digitally active." For instance, if "Business Owners" have the largest slice, it suggests their work or lifestyle involves heavy phone usage.
- **Metric**: The percentages shown are the relative average hours spent on phones per day for each group.

## 2. Device Type Distribution (Bar Chart)
- **File Name**: `viz2_device_distribution.png`
- **What it shows**: A count of users for each device category (Smartphone, Tablet, Desktop, etc.).
- **Key Insight**: This reveals the most popular platform for social media consumption in the dataset. A high bar for "Smartphone" compared to "Tablet" indicates a mobile-first user base.
- **Metric**: The Y-axis represents the raw frequency (count) of users.

## 3. Trend of Daily Phone Hours by Age (Line Chart)
- **File Name**: `viz3_age_trend.png`
- **What it shows**: A trend line showing how average daily phone usage changes as user age increases.
- **Key Insight**: This chart identifies age-related patterns. For example, a downward slope might indicate that younger users spend significantly more time on their phones than older users, or there might be specific "peaks" at certain life stages (e.g., college age).
- **Metric**: The X-axis is Age, and the Y-axis is the Average Daily Phone Hours.

## 4. Correlation Heatmap of Numerical Features
- **File Name**: `viz4_correlation_heatmap.png`
- **What it shows**: A matrix showing the correlation coefficients between variables like Phone Hours, Social Media Hours, Sleep Hours, and Stress Level.
- **Key Insight**: 
    - **Positive Correlation (Red/Warm)**: Indicates that as one variable increases, the other tends to increase (e.g., Social Media Hours vs. Stress Level).
    - **Negative Correlation (Blue/Cool)**: Indicates that as one variable increases, the other decreases (e.g., Phone Hours vs. Sleep Hours).
    - **Values near 0**: Indicate little to no linear relationship.
- **Metric**: Coefficients range from -1.0 to +1.0.

## 5. Confusion Matrix: High Stress Prediction (Heatmap)
- **File Name**: `viz5_classification_confusion_matrix.png`
- **What it shows**: A visual representation of the Classification Model's performance in predicting "High Stress" vs "Low Stress".
- **Key Insight**: 
    - **True Positives (Bottom Right)**: Correctly predicted "High Stress".
    - **True Negatives (Top Left)**: Correctly predicted "Low Stress".
    - **False Positives/Negatives**: Shows where the model is making errors.
- **Metric**: Raw counts of predictions in each category.
